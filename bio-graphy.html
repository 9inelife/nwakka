About Emma Nwaka

Born into a simple family in a quiet village in Amokwe-Amiyi, Ogudasa in Isuikwuato Local Government Area of Abia State, Emma Nwaka has always believed that one’s life’s trajectory is shaped by the mind, strong will and a grounded belief in God.

Education

One could say that Emma Nwaka is no stranger to opposition and life’s challenges. As a young man, one of the first walls of resistance that he had to scale was access to education. One of his favourite stories to tell is when he was offered admission to read Law at the prestigious University of Nigeria, Nsukka. The admission came when he had just started studying journalism and after successfully passing JAMB of which he was one of the first sets in 1978. With zero money in his pocket and the lack of financial support from his parents, Emma recalls going to meet with the university registrar to plead for a partial refund of his journalism fees to enable him to proceed to study Law at the University of Nigeria. The Registrar told him point blank that fees are non-refundable. Young Emma

Nwaka broke down in tears and Mr. Ajayi, the registrar, had pity on him and gave him some money. It was about 30 years later that Senator Nwaka found out that Mr. Ajayi had actually refunded him out of his own personal funds.

Early Career
After his national youth service at the Ministry of Justice, Kano, Emma Nwaka joined the Law Firm of the Late Chief James Uba in Aba in 1984. In 1987, he went solo under the rubric of Okaome Law Firm. Okaome simply means one whose word is his bond. And any person who has had any dealings with Emma Nwaka will attest that his life embodies this name.

When the Nigerian Military President, General Babangida sounded the clarion call for the new breed to show interest in politics, Emma Nwaka took the bait. He joined the National Republican Convention and became the first elected St at e Legal Adviser of the party in the old Imo State. When Abia was carved out of the Imo St at e in 1991, Emma Nwaka disengaged as Legal Adviser and successfully ran for the Senate where he became at 34 years of age one of the youngest members of the red chamber. His senate years were cut short by General Abacha' s coup of 1993. Following this, Emma Nwaka returned to his law chamber.

Politics

Emma has carried this grit, mental fortitude and belief in the benevolence of society through law school, to becoming the first selected State Legal Advisor in the National Republican Convention under old Imo State, to being one of the youngest members of the Senate at 34, to joining the People’s Democratic Party where he has served under various capacities as a State commissioner and Party Chairman and to seating on the boards of both private and public institutions.

His life experiences have taught him to look into the eyes of every young Nigerian and see value and potential; the same way Mr. Ajayi looked at him. The wisdom and knowledge he has attained in his journey of life makes him look up in gratitude to the older generation who believed in him and gave him a ladder to climb. He desires to ensure that as Governor of Abia, all spheres of society in Abia, the young and old, will be given the opportunity achieve their greatest heights by improving education, strengthening governance, boosting commerce & trade, reinforcing security and imbibing patriotism in the hearts of the young and old.

Emma Nwaka’s desire to become the next Governor of Abia State is not born out of ambition. It is an opportunity to roll up his sleeve to serve, work and rebuild God’s Own State of Abia.
